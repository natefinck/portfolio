Social Media: A Two Sided Coin

August 23, 2021

In my middle and high school years, I must admit I was a fanatic. Snapchat streaks, Instagram likes and Twitter retweets; they pretty much shaped everything I did. To the point where I almost skipped a school trip because we had to turn in our phones (I didn’t want to lose my 700-day Snapchat streak). Luckily, this was pretty much the first time I said enough is enough. I gave up social media for lent my junior year of high school, and I’ve been mindful of my screen time ever since.

And I’ve come a long way! My screen time is still more than I’d like, but it gets pretty difficult when so many of our everyday needs come through our phone. I average about 2-3 hours on my cell phone per day, and it seems I am doing pretty good compared to the results of this survey on college students at Ohio University. I like both Instagram and Facebook because they allow me to keep up with the people I don’t see often. Unfortunately, it seems they’re both filled with suggested posts and ads that grab my attention, but don’t necessarily interest me. Regardless, they fill my time. In fact, time is the main reason I don’t have Twitter or TikTok. When I had them before, obviously, I enjoyed them a lot. Too much, however; I ended up deleting them both a few weeks after downloading.

I don’t like how social media has become a social status game. Sure, I post on Instagram, but my posts are pretty dumb (Seriously, my last few posts made no sense to an outsider). I use the app for fun – posting photos that are fun to post. Instagram has become a platform to show off your best self, and I think it’s especially fake.

That isn’t even the worst of social media. Many issues – surveillance, threat to democracy, and censorship among others – plague social media. I believe the most important issue, though, is the link between social media addiction and depression . It’s truly a shame that so many people, especially youth, are drug into social media unknowingly and suffer the harsh effects that it has.

Social media can be powerful, and as a student of media I understand its potential. That’s why I made this website, as a way to get myself and my work out there to those who may be interested. I know that as long as I’m being genuine and professional online, I’ll stay out of trouble.
