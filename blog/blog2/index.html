One-Minute Summary

August 30, 2021

Initially, it seems like an easy task. It’s just one sentence. I could likely write books upon books about my life. Yet I find it difficult to just come up with some adjectives that describe me and the person I strive to be. If I had to choose one word, I’d choose ‘thoughtful.’ Yet, I should to come up with a clear and concise sentence, as explained by Daniel Pink .

As I reflect on my life, the things I value, and the goals that I have, trying to sum it up reminds me of a quote by an idol of mine:

“Simple can be harder than complex. You have to work hard to get your thinking clean to make it simple”

– Steve Jobs.

(I’m not sure he thought hard about this quote – it seems a little complex)

Although Steve was talking about designing a premium mobile phone , I get reminded of the quote often, especially as I design graphics, programs, videos, and websites (and, as I craft my sentence). Some deep self-reflection is necessary.

I hate describing myself because I am a top tier human and I dislike bragging, not to brag. The past year and a half have been filled with a lot of alone time in my hometown of Falls City, Nebraska. I love it there. Through this, I became a lot quieter and more thoughtful in my actions. I used to consider myself an extrovert in high school, but today I would say I’m just the opposite. I recognize kindness in the people around me and try my best to be kind in return.

We'll go with this:

I am thoughtful in the way I treat people.
